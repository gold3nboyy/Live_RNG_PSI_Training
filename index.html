About this generator

How it works

This generator produces a continuous stream of hardware‑based random numbers, similar to the noise sources used in scientific random event generators. Each number is independent and unpredictable, allowing you to observe natural fluctuations without algorithmic bias. The system is intentionally minimalistic to reduce cognitive load and support meditative or experimental use. By watching the live stream, you can explore how randomness behaves under different mental states.

Background and research

The concept is inspired by decades of mind–matter research at institutions such as the PEAR Lab and SRI International. These studies explored whether focused intention, emotional coherence, or meditative states could correlate with subtle statistical shifts in random systems. Results are debated, but modern work continues to investigate how mindset and physiological states influence perception of randomness. This generator provides a transparent way to explore similar ideas personally.

Disclaimer

This tool does not claim to demonstrate or validate any paranormal ability. All deviations you observe can be explained through normal statistical variation, cognitive bias, or psychological factors such as expectation and pattern seeking. The generator is intended for personal exploration, mindfulness practice, and curiosity‑driven experimentation. Use it as a reflective tool rather than a diagnostic instrument. This tool is resource‑efficient and runs entirely in your browser. It places only a moderate load on your system, even during extended sessions. It's a work in progress, so please forgive any bugs or problems, I'm still testing and exploring, new to vibecoding.

Features overview

Live number stream: Generates numbers continuously between Min and Max, allowing you to observe real‑time fluctuations. Because the randomness is hardware‑based, patterns arise naturally rather than through algorithmic bias.

History (Last 100): Shows the latest 100 numbers with streak colors to highlight repetition strength. This helps visualize clustering and natural variance in random systems.

Dashboard (Detailed explanation)

Entropy: Entropy describes how unpredictable the generated numbers are. A high entropy value means the results vary widely and no single number dominates the distribution. A low entropy value indicates that certain numbers appear more frequently, reducing randomness. This metric helps you understand whether the generator is producing a broad or narrow range of outcomes.

Chi² p‑value: The Chi-square p-value shows how closely the distribution of generated numbers matches a perfectly even random distribution. A high p-value means the numbers appear balanced and statistically uniform. A low p-value suggests that some numbers occur more often than expected, revealing patterns or irregularities. This value is useful for judging the statistical quality of the randomness.

Deviation: Deviation measures how far the actual distribution of numbers differs from an ideal uniform distribution. A high percentage means the frequencies of individual numbers vary significantly. A low percentage indicates that the numbers appear evenly and consistently. This metric complements the Chi-square p-value by giving you an intuitive sense of distribution balance.

Totals: Counts how many numbers have been generated in the session. A higher total increases statistical reliability and shows how long you’ve been running the generator.

Repeating & Streaks: Tracks how often identical numbers appear consecutively or dominate the dataset. Streaks are normal in randomness but often feel meaningful.

Heatmap: Displays the most frequent numbers as colored bars. Longer bars indicate dominance and help identify recurring values or potential biases.

Chart: Plots either the last 100 numbers (in Last 100 mode) or the last 500 numbers of the entire session (in Session mode). This provides a clear visual of short‑term fluctuations or long‑term trends depending on the selected Dashboard Mode.

Modes and examples

High Numbers: Highlights values above your threshold. Useful for exploring intention toward higher outcomes.

Low Numbers: Highlights values below your threshold. Ideal for exploring downward intention.

Target Number: Highlights only the chosen number. Designed for PSI focus training.

Street Mode: Highlights repeating sequences of identical numbers. Streaks are natural but often feel meaningful.

How to use for PSI training

Modern studies on attention, meditation, and altered states suggest that mindset strongly influences how people perceive randomness. Many experiments recommend entering a lightly altered state characterized by relaxed focus, reduced internal chatter, and heightened sensory awareness. As the generator begins producing numbers and you observe them, maintain a soft, non forceful intention—this mirrors protocols used in modern psi and consciousness studies. You are not generating the numbers yourself; instead, you are exploring whether your inner state correlates with how the random output behaves. Observe the stream without trying to force control; let your attention rest gently on the numbers. Over time, you may notice patterns, streaks, or shifts in distribution that feel meaningful or interesting. Whether these arise from cognitive bias, emotional resonance, or genuine statistical fluctuation, the process can be introspective and grounding. Use the dashboard metrics to track changes during different mental states, such as deep relaxation, focused intention, or creative flow. The goal is not to prove anything but to explore how your inner state interacts with your perception of randomness.
